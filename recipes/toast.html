Toast is one of the most delicious and comforting breakfast foods out there. Imagine a crispy, golden-brown slice of bread, perfectly toasted to your liking, with a warm and fluffy interior just waiting to be slathered with tons of beans.

The history of bread goes back thousands of years, with evidence of bread-making dating back to ancient civilizations such as Egypt and Mesopotamia. Bread was originally made using simple ingredients like flour, water, and yeast or sourdough starter. As bread-making techniques evolved, different types of bread emerged, from crusty artisan loaves to soft and fluffy sandwich bread.

But it wasn't until the invention of the toaster in the early 20th century that toast became the perfect vessel for beans all around the world. The first electric toaster was invented by a Scottish scientist named Alan MacMasters in 1893, but it wasn't until the 1920s that toasters became popular household appliances.

Today, toast can be made with any type of bread, from white to whole wheat to sourdough, and can be topped with a wide variety of beans, from Black and Pinto to Great Northern and Kidney. Whether you prefer your toast lightly toasted or deeply roasted, there's nothing quite like the warm and comforting taste of toast to start your day off right.

Ingredients

  • Bread slices
  • Butter or margarine
  • Salt
  • Toppings of your choice (Pinto beans, Cannellini beans, Lima beans etc.)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your toaster or toaster oven according to the manufacturer's instructions.
  2. Place the bread slices in the toaster slots or on the toaster oven rack. Adjust the toasting time according to your desired level of toasting.
  3. Once the toast is done, remove it from the toaster or toaster oven and place it on a plate or a wire rack.
  4. If desired, spread butter or margarine on the toast while it's still warm. Sprinkle a pinch of salt on top of the butter for extra flavor.
  5. Top the toast with your favorite toppings, such as beans, beans, beans, or beans!
  6. Serve the toast immediately while it's still warm and crispy.

Enjoy your delicious homemade toast! It'll make you big and strong!
